The Case for Renewable Energy in Indonesia

March 27, 2026Own Post
Renewable EnergyIndonesiaEnergy Transition

Indonesia stands at a critical crossroads in its energy development. As the world’s fourth most populous country and one of the fastest-growing economies in Southeast Asia, the decisions made today about energy infrastructure will shape the nation’s trajectory for decades.

The Current Energy Mix

Coal continues to dominate Indonesia’s electricity generation, accounting for over 60% of the national power mix. While this has provided affordable electricity for industrial growth, it comes at a significant environmental and health cost.

The Opportunity

Indonesia is blessed with extraordinary renewable energy potential. With over 17,000 islands straddling the equator, the country receives abundant solar irradiance year-round. Its mountainous terrain offers excellent hydropower potential, while its coastal geography enables offshore wind and ocean energy.

A recent IRENA report shows that the cost of solar and wind power has fallen by more than 80% over the past decade, making renewables the cheapest source of new electricity in most of the world.

The Path Forward

Investing in domestic renewable capacity reduces fuel import dependence, creates local employment, and positions Indonesia as a leader in the emerging green economy. With the right policy frameworks and international partnerships, Indonesia can achieve a just and rapid energy transition that benefits all its citizens.
